               the desirability of their genes, eugenics is widely considered unethical.[0m
[36m         LONG  The term eugenics was coined in 1883 by Francis Galton, a cousin of Charles Darwin, and it comes from the Greek roots eu- "good" and genos "birth." Galton
               believed that the human race could be improved by encouraging people who have "good" genes to marry early and have lots of children, and
               discouraging people with "bad" genes from procreating at all. Nazi Germany provided a horrifying example of such a program at work, and
               eugenics is now seen as abhorrent.[0m
