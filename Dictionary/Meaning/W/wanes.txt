                                                                                      WANES                                                                                       
  SHORT        Things that wax and wane grow larger and smaller, like the moon. Things that wane simply grow smaller. "My initial enthusiasm for helping waned when I saw the
               pile of envelopes that needed licking."
  LONG         You may have heard the expression "on the wane." It means a fad, or a fashion, or a disease, that is on its way out the door. "The face mask fad at school was
               on the wane as new reports of kids infected with a dreaded new fly virus waned."
                                                                            EXAMPLES FROM VOCABULARY:                                                                             
     >         As American influence wanes in East Asia, expect more countries to follow the Philippines and Malaysia and jump on the Beijing bandwagon.
     >         The belief in this fourth pillar waxes and wanes, but is always present.
     >         The recently created position, created in a 2004 law, waxes and wanes in power with the personality of its occupant.
     >         Their dominance in fourth quarters wanes in close games.
     >         Sales of new cars and trucks were expected to fall in October as consumer demand wanes.

 1   (Of The Moon) Have A Progressively Smaller Part Of Its Visible Surface Illuminated, So That It Appears To Decrease In Size.
	 Eg.  "The Moon Is Waning"
	 Synonyms:  disappear , decrease , diminish , dwindle , 
	 Antonyms:  wax , 

 2   (Of A State Or Feeling) Decrease In Vigour Or Extent; Become Weaker.
	 Eg.  "Confidence In The Dollar Waned"
	 Eg.  "Support For The Strike Was Waning"
	 Synonyms:  decrease , decline , diminish , dwindle , shrink , 
	 Antonyms:  increase , grow , 

 3   The Amount By Which A Plank Or Log Is Bevelled Or Falls Short Of A Squared Shape.
	 Eg.  "I Cut The Log Into Slabs Without Removing The Outside Wane"
