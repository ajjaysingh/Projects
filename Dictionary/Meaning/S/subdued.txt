                                                                                     SUBDUED                                                                                      
  SHORT        The adjective subdued refers to something, like a sound, that has a lowered intensity. Your loud conversation with a friend in the back of the classroom is
               likely to become subdued when the teacher passes out the exams.
  LONG         The word subdued is related to the Latin word, subdere, which means "to subtract from." Something that is subdued has lost some of its strength or intensity.
               Subdued lighting might create a romantic mood at a restaurant. And a subdued child is one who is no longer having a fit. A subdued economy is
               probably pretty sluggish.
                                                                            EXAMPLES FROM VOCABULARY:                                                                             
     >         The normally buoyant capital has been a notably subdued place since Castro’s death was announced Friday.
     >         Cuba’s government declared nine days of national mourning after Castro died and this normally vibrant city has been notably subdued.
     >         Lined up outdoors at the Coppelia ice cream parlor in downtown Havana, conversations were more subdued than the animated tones for which Cubans are famed.
     >         Apart from the chanting students, Havana life went on largely as normal, only quieter and more subdued following the news of Castro's death.
     >         He could not miss in the fourth, racing through the set with a barrage of spectacular shots against a subdued Cilic.

 1   (Of A Person Or Their Manner) Quiet And Rather Reflective Or Depressed.
	 Eg.  "I Felt Strangely Subdued As I Drove Home"
	 Eg.  "Lewis'S Subdued Air Had Changed To One Of High Good Humour"
	 Eg.  "They Chatted In Subdued Tones"
	 Synonyms:  sombre , low-spirited , downcast , sad , dejected , 
	 Antonyms:  lively , cheerful , loud , noisy , 

 2   (Of Colour Or Lighting) Soft And Restrained.
	 Eg.  "A Subdued Glow Came Through The Curtains"
	 Eg.  "The Subdued Light Made Mary Appear Pale"
	 Synonyms:  dim , muted , soft , low-key , subtle , 
	 Antonyms:  bright , 

 3   Overcome, Quieten, Or Bring Under Control (A Feeling Or Person).
	 Eg.  "She Managed To Subdue An Instinct To Applaud"
	 Eg.  "He Is Said To Have Slain Or Subdued All Those Who Had Plotted Against Him"
	 Eg.  "She Could Not Subdue Her Longing For Praise"
	 Synonyms:  conquer , defeat , vanquish , overpower , overcome , 

 4   Bring (A Country Or People) Under Control By Force.
	 Eg.  "Charles Went On A Campaign To Subdue The Saxons"
