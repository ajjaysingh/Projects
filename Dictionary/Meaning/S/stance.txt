                                                                                      STANCE                                                                                      
  SHORT        Your stance is your posture or the way you stand. Figuratively, if you take a stance against bullying, you are standing against it.
  LONG         If you take a stance on a contentious issue, it means you believe strongly about it one way or the other. If your stance is unpopular, you'll need some
               courage to speak your mind. The word comes from the Italian stanza which means stopping place. Your stance is something that's not likely to
               change. You have stopped there, your decision is made. You're done.

 1   The Way In Which Someone Stands, Especially When Deliberately Adopted (As In Cricket, Golf, And Other Sports); A Person'S Posture.
	 Eg.  "She Altered Her Stance, Resting All Her Weight On One Leg"
	 Eg.  "The Kick Is Performed By Starting From A Natural Stance And Bringing Up The Right Leg"
	 Synonyms:  posture , pose , attitude , bearing , 

 2   The Attitude Of A Person Or Organization Towards Something; A Standpoint.
	 Eg.  "The Party Is Changing Its Stance On Europe"
	 Eg.  "It Is Possible For Individual Teachers To Take A More Liberal Stance"
	 Synonyms:  attitude , stand , point of view , viewpoint , opinion , 

 3   A Site On A Street For A Market, Street Vendor'S Stall, Or Taxi Rank.

 4   A Ledge Or Foothold On Which A Belay Can Be Secured.
