                                                                                    TRANSPIRE                                                                                     
  SHORT        Transpire is a fancy way of saying "happen." You might go to a fortune teller to find out what will transpire in the future.
  LONG         Originally used to express when information became known or came to light, many purists will tell you that's really the best way to use transpire. It can also
               mean releasing vapor into the air, like when a plant transpires water through its leaves on a hot day. Note: you'll usually encounter this word
               in its past tense: for example, "We had to watch the replay to figure out what had transpired."
                                                                            EXAMPLES FROM VOCABULARY:                                                                             
     >         Neither side generated any quality opportunities in the first 45 minutes, though as the half transpired, the Hoyas began to find their way.
     >         Still, Orgeron said he went to bed Friday night unsure of what would transpire.
     >         “As time transpires, Fredericksburg isn’t as sleepy as 20 years ago, and it keeps growing,” said Bates.
     >         Something unusual is transpiring here, on the outskirts of big-time football.
     >         Discussing what transpired several years ago, he was again the Senator: speaking deliberately, using three-dollar words, filibustering over an admittedly
               difficult topic.

 1   (Of A Secret Or Something Unknown) Come To Be Known; Be Revealed.
	 Eg.  "It Transpired That Millions Of Dollars Of Debt Had Been Hidden In A Complex Web Of Transactions"
	 Eg.  "It Transpired That Mr. Washington Had Been In London Throughout The Period Of The Robberies"
	 Synonyms:  emerge , materialize , 

 2   Prove To Be The Case.
	 Eg.  "As It Transpired, He Was Right"

 3   Occur; Happen.
	 Eg.  "I'M Going To Find Out Exactly What Transpired"
	 Eg.  "The Visitors Explained Where They Had Come From And What Had Transpired There"
	 Synonyms:  happen , occur , arise , chance , ensue , 

 4   (Of A Plant Or Leaf) Give Off Water Vapour Through The Stomata.
	 Eg.  "A Cactus Does Not Transpire As Freely As Most Plants"
