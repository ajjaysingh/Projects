                                                                                     INTRIGUE                                                                                     
  SHORT        An intrigue is a secret plot. If you ever become the monarch of a small island kingdom, keep watch for signs of any intrigue against you.
  LONG         Intrigue comes from the Latin verb intricare,  to entangle, and is related to intricate. It can be a noun, meaning underhanded plot, or a verb for the act of
               plotting. Agents of two opposing powers intrigue against each other. In the late nineteenth century it also came to mean the feeling of
               curiosity or interest. If someone's ideas intrigue you, you want to know more about them.
