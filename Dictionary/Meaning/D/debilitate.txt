               debilitate bakeries' business.[0m
[36m         LONG  The verb debilitate traces back to the Latin word debilis, meaning “lame, disabled, crippled.” It’s often used to describe what disability or illness does to
               a person's health, but it can describe anything that has been weakened, like the sense of community that is slowly debilitated by people
               working longer hours and the lack of sidewalks that in many towns make it harder to walk around and meet the neighbors.[0m
