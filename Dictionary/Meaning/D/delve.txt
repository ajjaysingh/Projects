                                                                                      DELVE                                                                                       
  SHORT        The verb delve means to dig into, loosen, or investigate. She delved into her family's history and discovered an inventor, a checkers champion, and a circus
               equestrian in her ancestry.
  LONG         Delve has a literal meaning of to dig into the earth ("to delve the soil," as in preparing a garden, for example), but this sense is rare now. The verb is
               most often used in the more figurative sense — like to delve into a problem or into someone's personal life.
