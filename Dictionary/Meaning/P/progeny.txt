                                                                                     PROGENY                                                                                      
  SHORT        Progeny means "offspring" or "children." You and your brothers are the progeny of your parents, and your cat's new litter of kittens is her progeny.
  LONG         Synonyms for progeny include descendants, product, and offspring, so you're also your grandparents' and great-grandparents' progeny. And, if your pet goat has
               babies every spring, you'll get to raise dozens of her progeny. Plants have progeny too — blow the fluffy seeds of a dandelion in your yard and
               its progeny can multiply, summer after summer, until your lawn is full of cheerful yellow flowers.
