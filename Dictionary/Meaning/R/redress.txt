                                                                                     REDRESS                                                                                      
  SHORT        The verb redress is used when you are supposed to fix a problem and make amends. You want your parents to redress the fact that you don't have a pet. Your
               parents offer to get a hamster, but instead, you say you want a monkey.
  LONG         Redress can be used as both a noun and a verb. In the noun form, it is the compensation for setting something right. As a verb it means to correct, right a
               wrong, or make restitution for something. The union organizers wanted the company to redress the fact that workers weren't getting lunch
               breaks.
                                                                            EXAMPLES FROM VOCABULARY:                                                                             
     >         The Consumer Financial Protection Bureau has nearly replaced the Better Business Bureau as the first stop for dissatisfied customers seeking redress.
     >         He seeks redress at “the Gate,” where thousands stand in line hoping to meet someone in authority.
     >         And they have every right to petition for the redress of their grievances.
     >         But can oysters begin to help redress the natural balance?
     >         But unconventional friendships are being formed by some of those trying to redress the balance.

 1   Remedy Or Set Right (An Undesirable Or Unfair Situation).
	 Eg.  "The Question Is How To Redress The Consequences Of Racist Land Policies"
	 Eg.  "People No Longer Take To The Barricades To Redress Wrongs"
	 Eg.  "Bad News Is Drowning Out The Good News, But We Aim To Redress The Balance"
	 Synonyms:  rectify , correct , right , amend , remedy , 

 2   Set Upright Again.
	 Eg.  "Some Ambitious Architect Being Called To Redress A Leaning Wall"

 3   Remedy Or Compensation For A Wrong Or Grievance.
	 Eg.  "Those Seeking Redress For An Infringement Of Public Law Rights"
	 Eg.  "Their Best Hope Of Redress Lay In Court Action"
	 Synonyms:  compensation , reparation , restitution , recompense , repayment ,
