                                                                                     REPRIEVE                                                                                     
  SHORT        A reprieve is a break in or cancellation of a painful or otherwise lousy situation. If you're being tortured, a reprieve is a break from whatever's tormenting
               you.
  LONG         For some, a night at the opera is a night of punishing boredom during which the only reprieve is the intermission. For others, it's baseball that feels like
               torture. The seventh-inning stretch is the only reprieve from a night of endless waiting. Often, you'll hear reprieve used when a court or
               governor decides not to execute a prisoner. That prisoner is given a reprieve and will be allowed to live. He probably won't get a reprieve
               from prison, though, unless he's found innocent.
                                                                            EXAMPLES FROM VOCABULARY:                                                                             
     >         If you don’t receive it, I will have been reprieved.
     >         Even Thanksgiving did not provide a reprieve from the extraordinary public efforts to cast doubt on Mr. Romney.
     >         Hampshire remain in the top flight after being reprieved by the ECB, which relegated Durham after the county accepted a £3.8m financial aid package.
     >         But their reprieve came just one day after word that the pardoned turkey alumni group has shrunk in the past year.
     >         Shadows stretch for yards by 3 p.m., and by 5, the cold returns and the reprieve from winter is over.

 1   Cancel Or Postpone The Punishment Of (Someone, Especially Someone Condemned To Death).
	 Eg.  "Under The New Regime, Prisoners Under Sentence Of Death Were Reprieved"
	 Eg.  "She Was Sentenced To Death, But Was Reprieved"
	 Synonyms:  pardon , spare , acquit , amnesty , respite , 
	 Antonyms:  charge , punish , 

 2   Abandon Or Postpone Plans To Close Or Abolish (Something).
	 Eg.  "The Threatened Pits Could Be Reprieved"
	 Eg.  "The Accident And Emergency Unit Has Also Been Reprieved"
	 Synonyms:  save , rescue , 

 3   A Cancellation Or Postponement Of A Punishment.
	 Eg.  "He Accepted The Death Sentence And Refused To Appeal For A Reprieve"
	 Eg.  "He Was Saved By A Last-Minute Reprieve"
	 Synonyms:  remission , respite , pardon , amnesty , acquittal , 

 4   A Cancellation Or Postponement Of An Undesirable Event.
	 Eg.  "A Mother Who Faced Eviction Has Been Given A Reprieve"
