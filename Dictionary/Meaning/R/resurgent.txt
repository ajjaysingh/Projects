                                                                                    RESURGENT                                                                                     
  SHORT        Something resurgent comes back to life or is reinvigorated. An old song's popularity might be resurgent after it's featured on the soundtrack of a popular new
               movie.
  LONG         When things fall out of favor or stop being active and then experience a burst of energy or interest, they're resurgent. After a period of very conservative
               political control, resurgent liberal beliefs could lead to a progressive president being elected. And if people get bored with new technology
               and gadgets, there may be a resurgent interest in hardcover books and Polaroid cameras. In Latin, resurgere means "rise again."
                                                                            EXAMPLES FROM VOCABULARY:                                                                             
     >         The stock market is reaching record highs, and sales of Picassos and Warhols are resurgent.
     >         But the company sees an upturn in South America propelled by resurgent sales of farm equipment in Brazil and Argentina.
     >         But the methodical Merkel will find herself swimming against the tide of resurgent nationalism, including in Germany.
     >         There the conservative challenger would in all likelihood face the leader of the resurgent far-right National Front party, Marine Le Pen.
     >         And the resurgent Trojans, left for dead two months ago, now have two potential routes to the Rose Bowl on Jan. 2.

 1   Increasing Or Reviving After A Period Of Little Activity, Popularity, Or Occurrence.
