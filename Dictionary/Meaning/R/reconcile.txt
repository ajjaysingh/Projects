                                                                                    RECONCILE                                                                                     
  SHORT        Reach for the verb reconcile to make different things come together or resolve a matter.
  LONG         If you've ever had an argument with someone and then made up, you have reconciled. Reconcile is a verb that can mean "to become resigned" like if you're
               reconciled to getting the bottom bunk when you wanted the top. It also means to "bring into agreement and harmony." Words like it are pacify,
               harmonize, and accommodate — reconcile is definitely an agreeable word!
                                                                            EXAMPLES FROM VOCABULARY:                                                                             
     >         Washington state needs to reconcile those disturbing patterns, because they are linked.
     >         His death was met with acerbic statements from members of Congress who have harshly criticized Mr. Obama’s efforts to reconcile with Cuba.
     >         He loves them, sees them regularly, and intends to reconcile with his ex-wife.
     >         Homer and Barney eventually reconcile, proclaiming that not even God can stop them now.
     >         Ms. Sanchez said that Ms. Manu had apologized, and the two are working on reconciling.

 1   Restore Friendly Relations Between.
	 Eg.  "The King And The Archbishop Were Publicly Reconciled"

 2   Settle (A Quarrel).
	 Eg.  "Advice On How To Reconcile The Conflict"
	 Eg.  "The News Reconciled Us"
	 Eg.  "The Quarrel Was Reconciled"
	 Synonyms:  reunite , pacify , appease , placate , propitiate , 
	 Antonyms:  estrange , alienate , 

 3   Make Or Show To Be Compatible.
	 Eg.  "The Agreement Had To Be Reconciled With The City'S New International Relations Policy"
	 Eg.  "It Wasn'T Easy Trying To Reconcile His Religious Beliefs With His Career"
	 Synonyms:  harmonize , square , synthesize , adjust , balance , 

 4   Make Someone Accept (A Disagreeable Or Unwelcome Thing).
	 Eg.  "He Was Reconciled To Leaving"
	 Eg.  "The Creditors Had To Reconcile Themselves To Drastic Losses Of Income And Capital"
	 Synonyms:  accept , 

 5   Make (One Account) Consistent With Another, Especially By Allowing For Transactions Begun But Not Yet Completed.
	 Eg.  "It Is Not Necessary To Reconcile The Cost Accounts To The Financial Accounts"
