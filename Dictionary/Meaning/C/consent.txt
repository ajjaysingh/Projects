                                                                                     CONSENT                                                                                      
  SHORT        Before you have surgery, you'd better give your consent, because the surgeon can't get his scalpel anywhere near your skin without first getting this
               permission from you.
  LONG         Consent is permission that can be given or taken away. A father might give his consent to allow a young man to marry his daughter, but he might refuse that
               consent if he learns that the prospective groom has a prison record. A homeowner who won't give a developer consent to tear down her house and
               build a shopping mall might finally consent after receiving a very large check from the developer.
                                                                            EXAMPLES FROM VOCABULARY:                                                                             
     >         “It is all about informed consent,” Melchiors said.
     >         The homeowner later gave officers consent to search the residence and officers found two more rifles and two shotguns.
     >         Scotland's Lord Advocate will argue at the Supreme Court that Holyrood's consent should be sought over legislation invoking Article 50.
     >         Dorothea Harvilik voluntarily surrendered her license as part of a consent order with the state Board of Nursing that resolves the allegations against her.
     >         Officials now argue that left untackled, Chinese dumping will kill the public consent needed to strike trade deals.

 1   Permission For Something To Happen Or Agreement To Do Something.
	 Eg.  "No Change May Be Made Without The Consent Of All The Partners"
	 Eg.  "A Change In The Rules Requires The Consent Of All Members"
	 Synonyms:  agreement , assent , accord , permission , authorization , 
	 Antonyms:  dissent , 

 2   Give Permission For Something To Happen.
	 Eg.  "He Consented To A Search By A Detective"
	 Eg.  "All The Patients Consented To Surgery"
	 Synonyms:  allow , sanction , accept , approve , 
	 Antonyms:  dissent , forbid , 

 3   Agree To Do Something.
	 Eg.  "He Had Consented To Serve As External Assessor On The Panel"
