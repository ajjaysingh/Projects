                                                                                     CALLOUS                                                                                      
  SHORT        A callous person is insensitive or emotionally hardened. If you laugh at your little sister while she's trying to show you her poetry, you're being callous.
  LONG         Callous comes from the Latin root callum for hard skin.  If you walk barefoot a lot, your feet will become calloused. We usually use callous in the
               metaphorical sense for emotionally hardened. If someone is unmoved by other people's problems, you might say he shows a callous indifference to
               human suffering.
                                                                            EXAMPLES FROM VOCABULARY:                                                                             
     >         War had been an inevitable outcome: an anonymous and ultimately callous economic system, decorated with flags and lies, had sent millions to their deaths.
     >         “In a callous display of indifference to human life, Mr. Pina sold others the same drugs that nearly killed him.”
     >         In any event, you'd think politicians would know better than to make such easily misinterpreted, callous remarks.
     >         “His callous behavior and willingness to sell the bodies of his young victims for his own financial gain is abhorrent.”
     >         Morally, they earned Negan’s retribution with their callous disregard for his people.

 1   Showing Or Having An Insensitive And Cruel Disregard For Others.
	 Eg.  "His Callous Comments About The Murder Made Me Shiver"
	 Eg.  "His Callous Disregard For The Feelings And Wishes Of Others"
	 Synonyms:  heartless , unfeeling , uncaring , cold , cold-hearted , 
	 Antonyms:  kind , compassionate , 

 2   Variant Spelling Of Callus.

 3   A Thickened And Hardened Part Of The Skin Or Soft Tissue, Especially In An Area That Has Been Subjected To Friction.

 4   The Bony Healing Tissue Which Forms Around The Ends Of Broken Bone.

 5   A Hard Formation Of Tissue, Especially New Tissue Formed Over A Wound.
	 Eg.  "The Exposed Surface Will Quickly Form Healing Callus"
