                                                                                       FRET                                                                                       
  SHORT        When you fret, you worry so much about something that it eats away at you. Many people fret about taking standardized tests, but really, they're nothing to
               sweat.
  LONG         Fret comes from the Old English word freton which means to devour like an animal. When you fret over something, it consumes your thoughts. If you tell your
               mother to not fret about you while you're at a sleepover camp, you're telling her to not worry about you too much. Sometimes it means to be
               agitated though. When you're waiting for the results of an exam, you might fret and wring your hands. In a totally unrelated meaning, a guitar
               player calls the raised lines on the neck of the guitar that help him play  correctly frets.

	 Eg.  "The Workers Fretted About Being Displaced By Machines"
	 Synonyms:  worry , agonize , anguish , sorrow , sigh , 

 2   Cause Anxiety To.
	 Eg.  "His Absence During Her Times Awake Began To Fret Her"
	 Eg.  "His Absence Began To Fret Her"
	 Synonyms:  trouble , bother , concern , perturb , disturb ,

 3   Gradually Wear Away (Something) By Rubbing Or Gnawing.
	 Eg.  "The Bay'S Black Waves Fret The Seafront"

 4   Form (A Channel Or Passage) By Rubbing Or Wearing Away.
	 Eg.  "What Shape The Sea Has Fretted Into The Land"

 5   Flow Or Move In Small Waves.
	 Eg.  "Squelchy Clay That Fretted Between His Toes"

 6   A State Of Anxiety.
	 Eg.  "An Economy In A State Of Fret And Irritation"

 8   A Device Of Narrow Diagonal Bands Interlaced Through A Diamond.
