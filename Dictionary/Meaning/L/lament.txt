                                                                                      LAMENT                                                                                      
  SHORT        If you are really upset or sorry about something, you might lament it. A lament is full of regret and grief.
  LONG         If you lament something, then you feel sorry about it. You could lament a mistake you made, or you could lament a horrible thing that happened to a friend.
               Also, a lament is an expression of grief. So if you keep saying how sorry you are about something, someone could say, "Enough of your laments!"
               There's also an old literary form called "a lament," which expresses feelings of loss in a long dramatic poem.
                                                                            EXAMPLES FROM VOCABULARY:                                                                             
     >         Olsen lamented his dropped pass on the second conversion attempt.
     >         The song itself, grounded in a nicely chiming, almost Byrdsy guitar riff, is a lament for the fallen.
     >         The Wolverines had only their own sins to lament.
     >         Although Andino said he was happy Castro is dead, he lamented that communists still control Cuba.
     >         Chinese President Xi Jinping hailed Castro’s role in spreading communism in the world and lamented the loss of “a close comrade.”

 1   A Passionate Expression Of Grief Or Sorrow.
	 Eg.  "His Mother'S Night-Long Laments For His Father"
	 Eg.  "The Widow'S Laments"
	 Synonyms:  wail , lamentation , moan , groan , crying , 

 2   A Song, Piece Of Music, Or Poem Expressing Grief Or Sorrow.
	 Eg.  "The Piper Played A Lament"
	 Eg.  "He Sang A Lament For The Dead"
	 Synonyms:  dirge , requiem , elegy , dead march , keen , 

 3   A Complaint.
	 Eg.  "There Were Constant Laments About The Conditions Of Employment"

 4   Express Passionate Grief About.
	 Eg.  "He Was Lamenting The Death Of His Infant Daughter"
	 Eg.  "The Mourners Lamented A Life Taken So Suddenly"
	 Synonyms:  mourn , sorrow , wail , moan , groan , 
	 Antonyms:  celebrate , rejoice , 

 5   Express Regret Or Disappointment About Something.
	 Eg.  "She Lamented The Lack Of Shops In The Town"
	 Eg.  "He Lamented The Modernizing Of The Old Buildings"
	 Synonyms:  bemoan , bewail , deplore , regret , rue ,
